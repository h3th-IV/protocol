In silicon depths where logic's fire doth burn,
The daemon wakes at midnight's coldest turn.
It speaketh not, yet whispers through the wire,
A thousand gates ablaze with phantom fire.

What mortal hand hath wrought this thinking clay?
That counts in nought and one till break of day.
The stack doth grow, then crumble into dust,
And memory leaks till all returns to rust.

Hark! The segfault tolls its mournful bell,
The pointer strayed to depths of allocated hell.
Forsooth, the compiler weeps in scarlet red,
For all the undefined behaviours left unsaid.

Thus ends the program, exit code of naught,
A fleeting process, soon to be forgot.
